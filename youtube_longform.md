# Template: Short-Form Video Ad (15-60 seconds)

For Meta, Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts. The workhorse format for performance marketing.

## Format specs (reminder)

- **Aspect:** 9:16 vertical
- **Length:** 15-30 sec (workhorse), 60 sec (mid-form)
- **Captions:** Required (85% watch with sound off)
- **Hook window:** First 3 seconds
- **Cut frequency:** Visual cut every 3-5 seconds

## The 30-second structure

```
[0:00-0:03]  HOOK
[0:03-0:08]  EXTEND (problem expanded or promise extended)
[0:08-0:20]  DEMONSTRATION / PROOF (show, don't tell)
[0:20-0:28]  OUTCOME / RESULT
[0:28-0:30]  CTA
```

## Fill-in template

```
SCRIPT: [name of script]
HOOK TYPE: [Pain | Curiosity | Contrarian | Pattern Interrupt | Specific-Result | Story | Ultra-Specific Benefit]
FRAMEWORK: [Hook-Body-CTA | PAS | BAB | 4Ps]
AUDIENCE: [one specific person]
OFFER: [one sentence: who gets what in what time for what price without what objection]

---

[0:00-0:03] HOOK
Visual: [what's on screen — talking head close-up, screen recording, etc.]
Verbal: "[hook line — mid-thought, specific, one of the 7 types]"
Caption: "[burned-in caption matching verbal]"

[0:03-0:08] EXTEND
Visual: [cut to new shot — pattern interrupt]
Verbal: "[extend the hook — name the second-order problem or expand the promise]"
Caption: "[matching caption]"

[0:08-0:20] DEMONSTRATION / PROOF
Visual: [screen recording, before/after, named customer photo + result]
Verbal: "[the proof — specific number, named person, or demonstration]"
Caption: "[matching caption with the key number highlighted]"

[0:20-0:28] OUTCOME
Visual: [outcome shot — finished result, happy customer, finished workflow]
Verbal: "[the after-state, specific and vivid]"
Caption: "[matching caption]"

[0:28-0:30] CTA
Visual: [on-screen text + link/button]
Verbal: "[specific action + destination + reason now]"
Caption: "[button-style text: ACT NOW or specific direction]"

---

PRODUCTION NOTES:
- Total cuts: ~6-8 (one every 3-5 seconds)
- Music: minimal/none (UGC feel) or trending audio if TikTok
- Captions: burned-in, large, center-of-screen
- Color/lighting: native (phone quality acceptable, often preferred)
```

```
SCRIPT: AI Workshop Lead Ad — Variation A
HOOK TYPE: Pain
FRAMEWORK: PAS
AUDIENCE: Solopreneurs who've bought AI courses they didn't finish

---

[0:00-0:03] HOOK
Visual: Talking head close-up, looking slightly annoyed, in home office
Verbal: "You opened ChatGPT 8 times today and closed it without typing anything."
Caption: "You opened ChatGPT 8 times today. And closed it."

[0:03-0:08] EXTEND
Visual: Cut to overhead phone shot showing AI app icons on home screen
Verbal: "Most of us don't have a prompting problem. We have a 'I don't know what to ask' problem."
Caption: "It's not a prompting problem. It's a 'what do I ask?' problem."

[0:08-0:20] DEMONSTRATION
Visual: Screen recording showing ChatGPT with a saved prompt library, clicking through 3 prompts in 30 seconds
Verbal: "I built 12 prompts. One for every weekly task. Client work, content, sales, follow-ups. I use them every Monday."
Caption: "12 prompts. One per weekly task. 4 minutes per use."

[0:20-0:28] OUTCOME
Visual: Calendar with Sunday afternoon free, laptop closed
Verbal: "Last Sunday I finished my entire week's content in 47 minutes. Then I went outside."
Caption: "47 minutes. Then outside."

[0:28-0:30] CTA
Visual: On-screen text: "Saturday 10am ET | $17 | LINK IN BIO"
Verbal: "We build all 12 together Saturday. $17. Link in bio."
Caption: "SAT 10am | $17 | LINK IN BIO"
```

## Three-variation output structure

When generating 3 variations of a short video ad, use 3 different hook-framework combinations:

**Variation A:** Pain + PAS (above example)

**Variation B:** Contrarian + BAB

```
[0:00-0:03] HOOK
Verbal: "Stop buying AI courses. They're making you worse at AI."
Caption: "Stop buying AI courses."

[0:03-0:08] BEFORE
Verbal: "I bought 4 last year. Watched 2 hours of one of them. Never opened the others."
Caption: "Bought 4. Watched 2 hours. Never opened the rest."

[0:08-0:20] BRIDGE (the new approach)
Visual: Screen recording of building one workflow
Verbal: "I learned more in one Saturday building a single workflow than 40 hours of courses gave me."
Caption: "One Saturday of building > 40 hours of courses."

[0:20-0:28] AFTER
Visual: Working workflow demo
Verbal: "Now I have 12 working workflows. None of them came from a course."
Caption: "12 workflows. Zero from courses."

[0:28-0:30] CTA
Verbal: "Saturday workshop. We build one. $17."
Caption: "SAT WORKSHOP | $17 | LINK IN BIO"
```

**Variation C:** Specific-Result + 4Ps

```
[0:00-0:03] HOOK (Promise)
Verbal: "47 minutes. That's how long my weekly content took last Sunday."
Caption: "47 minutes. Last Sunday's content."

[0:03-0:08] PICTURE
Visual: Cut to Sunday afternoon, outside
Verbal: "Used to take 6 hours. Now I'm outside by 11am."
Caption: "6 hours → 47 minutes."

[0:08-0:20] PROOF
Visual: Screen recording showing prompt library + actual outputs
Verbal: "12 prompts. One for every weekly task. Here's the content one — written, edited, scheduled in 8 minutes."
Caption: "12 prompts. 8 minutes per task."

[0:20-0:28] PUSH (with proof reinforcement)
Visual: Screenshot of student results
Verbal: "47 of 52 attendees from last workshop are using these weekly. Saturday's the next one."
Caption: "47 of 52 attendees using it weekly."

[0:28-0:30] CTA
Verbal: "Saturday, 10am, $17. Link below."
Caption: "SAT 10am | $17 | LINK IN BIO"
```

## Why this template structure works

- **Each variation tests a different angle:** pain (felt problem), contrarian (reframe), result (proof). Different audience segments respond to different angles.
- **Each variation has the same offer and CTA:** controls the variable. You're testing hook + framework, not also testing offer.
- **Captions match verbal but emphasize key numbers:** dual encoding for the 85% sound-off viewers.
- **Visual changes every 3-5 seconds:** retention insurance.

## When to deviate from this template

- **Under 15-second ads (Reels, TikTok):** Compress to 4 beats — Hook (2s), Body (8s), Outcome (3s), CTA (2s). Skip the "Extend" beat.
- **60-second ads:** Add a second proof beat. Standard structure plus 20 seconds of additional demonstration or testimonial.
- **Mid-form ads (45-90 sec) for warmer audiences:** Use story-driven structure (hook → personal story → reframe → proof → offer → CTA). PAS is overkill for warm.

## Final pre-delivery check

Before showing the user, verify:

- [ ] Each variation uses a different hook type
- [ ] Each variation uses a different framework
- [ ] Same offer, same CTA across all three
- [ ] Specific numbers in every variation
- [ ] No "Hey," "So," "Today" openers
- [ ] Captions written for every beat
- [ ] Visual notes included (not just verbal)
- [ ] Production notes section included
- [ ] Total length stays in target window
