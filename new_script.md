# Template: Webinar / Workshop Script (45-90 min)

The highest-converting format for $200-$5000 offers. Based on Russell Brunson's Perfect Webinar framework (refined over 15 years, $14M+ in ClickFunnels's first year).

## Format specs

- **Length:** 45-90 minutes (45 min sweet spot for B2B, 60-90 for course/coaching)
- **Format:** Slides + presenter on camera (small or PIP)
- **Captions:** Live captions recommended
- **Engagement:** Polls, chat questions, reactions every 10-15 min

## The critical engagement number

Webinars where attendees average **5-10 reactions/questions each** convert at **~69%**. Low-engagement webinars convert at **<20%**. That's a 3.5x difference driven entirely by interaction. The Q&A and chat are not the wrap-up — they're the conversion event.

## The 3-section structure

```
Section 1: Welcome + Origin Story        (10-15 min)
Section 2: The Three Secrets             (30-45 min)
Section 3: Stack & Close + Q&A           (15-30 min)
```

## Fill-in template

```
WEBINAR TITLE: "How to [result they want] without [thing they fear]"
EXAMPLE: "How to Ship Your First AI Workflow Without Learning Code"

AUDIENCE: [one specific person]
THE ONE THING: [the single transformation this webinar promises]
THE OFFER: [what you'll pitch at the end — price, format, deliverables]

THREE FALSE BELIEFS this audience holds:
1. [false belief #1 — usually about themselves, e.g., "I'm not technical enough"]
2. [false belief #2 — usually about the timing, e.g., "I need to learn more first"]
3. [false belief #3 — usually about the approach, e.g., "I need to do it the way everyone else does"]

---

## SECTION 1: WELCOME + ORIGIN STORY (10-15 minutes)

### 0:00-0:02 — Opening
- Welcome + housekeeping
- "Drop a 1 in the chat if you can hear me" (first trial close)
- Set expectations: "By the end of today you'll have [the One Thing]"
- Permission to ask questions throughout

### 0:02-0:05 — The Promise & Curiosity Loop
"Today we're going to cover [3 specific things]. The biggest one is at [timestamp] — that's the one that flipped it for me."
This loop keeps viewers watching for the payoff.

### 0:05-0:15 — Origin Story
Structure:
- Where you were (specific moment of struggle that audience will recognize)
- What you tried that didn't work
- The turning point (a specific moment of insight or external event)
- What changed
- "And that's why we're here today"

Goal: prove you've been where they are. Build trust through specificity.

Trial close at end: "Anyone else been here? Type 'yes' if this sounds familiar."

---

## SECTION 2: THE THREE SECRETS (30-45 minutes)

Each Secret breaks one false belief. Same structure for each:

### SECRET #1: [Title that promises the reframe]
Example: "It's not a technical problem — it's a 'where to start' problem"

Structure (10-12 minutes):
- **State the false belief publicly.** "Most of you are walking around believing X."
- **Tell a story of someone who believed it and was wrong.** Specific story, named (even if anonymized).
- **Show the new belief.** "Here's what's actually true."
- **Give one specific tactic.** Something they can use today, even if they don't buy.

Trial close: "Make sense so far? Type 'yes' if this lands."

### SECRET #2: [Title]
Example: "You don't need to learn AI first — you need to ship one workflow first"

Same structure (10-12 min). Story → false belief → new belief → tactic.

Trial close.

### SECRET #3: [Title]
Example: "Building it once for yourself > consuming 50 hours of courses"

Same structure (10-12 min). This is usually the most powerful Secret — save the biggest insight for last.

Trial close.

### Transition to offer (1-2 min)
"If you want to take what we covered today and actually ship it this week, here's what I want to offer you."

Important: don't apologize. Don't say "I hate this part." The transition should be confident.

---

## SECTION 3: STACK & CLOSE + Q&A (15-30 minutes)

### 0:45-0:50 — Introduce the Offer
"What you'll get when you join [program name]:"

Then THE STACK. Build value piece by piece BEFORE revealing the price:

```
- Component 1: [the main deliverable] — value: $___
- Component 2: [bonus or feature] — value: $___
- Component 3: [community / coaching access] — value: $___
- Component 4: [the proof / certification / artifact] — value: $___
- Component 5: [the lifetime piece or extra] — value: $___

Total value: $______
```

Each component must be REAL. Fake stacking destroys trust.

### 0:50-0:52 — Reveal Price
"Today's price: $____."

Pause. Let it land. The anchoring did the work.

### 0:52-0:55 — Risk Reversal
"Here's the guarantee: [specific terms]."

Make it concrete: "If you don't [specific outcome] within [specific time], I'll [specific refund or addition]."

### 0:55-1:00 — The CTA
"Here's exactly what to do. Click the link in the chat. Pick your option. I'll see you on the inside."

Repeat the CTA 2-3 times. Type the link in the chat yourself (don't rely on autoplay).

### 1:00-1:30+ — Q&A (THIS IS WHERE CONVERSION HAPPENS)

Don't truncate. Don't end at the scheduled time. The 30-50% of buyers who buy in Q&A need this time.

Q&A flow:
1. Take real questions from chat (read them aloud)
2. If chat is quiet, use FAQs: "A question I get a lot is..." then answer
3. Address common objections proactively: "If you're thinking 'I don't have time' — let me address that"
4. Show the CTA / link on screen the entire Q&A
5. Stay past the scheduled end. The last 20 minutes contain the most committed buyers.

### Standard FAQs to have ready
- "What if I'm a complete beginner?"
- "How long until I see results?"
- "What if I can't make the live sessions?"
- "How is this different from [competitor]?"
- "What's your refund policy in detail?"
- "Can I see [specific component] before I buy?"

Have a confident, specific answer to each. These convert.
```

## Webinar-specific rules

1. **Same script every week.** Refine over iterations. Don't rebuild each time.
2. **Build engagement deliberately.** Trial closes every 5-10 minutes. Polls at the 25%, 50%, 75% marks. Chat prompts before every Secret.
3. **Tease the offer in the first 5 minutes.** "By the end I'll show you how to do this 10x faster — stay till the end." This way the offer isn't a surprise.
4. **Don't apologize for the pitch.** Confidence in the offer is part of the offer.
5. **Stay past the scheduled end.** Buyers are the last to leave.
6. **Use the chat as a conversion tool.** Type the link yourself. Ask people to type "ready" or "in." Public commitments increase follow-through.
7. **Show the URL on every slide after the pitch starts.** Even if it's just a QR code in the corner.

## Webinar pitfalls (avoid)

- **90% teaching, 10% pitch.** Doesn't convert. 60-70% teaching, 30-40% offer + Q&A.
- **Surprise offer at minute 50.** Tease from the start.
- **Ending the moment the pitch ends.** Cuts off the Q&A buyers (30-50% of total sales).
- **Apologizing for the offer.** Signals the offer is bad.
- **Different content every week.** Never refines. Repetition is the path to optimization.
- **No trial closes.** The audience never enters "yes" mode.
