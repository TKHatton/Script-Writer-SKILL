# Anti-Patterns: What Not to Do

What to actively avoid in any script. Each item is paired with what to do instead. Refer to this when reviewing output or diagnosing why a script feels off.

## Anti-patterns by category

### Hooks that don't work

**❌ The "you won't believe" hook**
> *"You won't believe what happened when I tried this!"*

So overused it's now a meme. Brain rejects on sight.

**✅ Instead:**
> *"I tried this for 30 days. I'm furious at past me for not trying it sooner."*

---

**❌ The fake-secret hook**
> *"The one secret nobody is talking about..."*

Secret framing now signals low-quality. Audiences have built immunity.

**✅ Instead, name the specific belief:**
> *"Most people think you need a big audience first. The smallest list I ever built made the most money."*

---

**❌ Generic pain hook**
> *"Are you struggling with productivity?"*

Pain too vague. Filters for nobody.

**✅ Specific pain:**
> *"It's 11pm and you're still building the client report. You quoted three days ago and you still don't have a price."*

---

**❌ Generic story opener**
> *"Once upon a time I was a struggling entrepreneur..."*

Brain pattern-matches to "long boring story incoming."

**✅ Mid-story open:**
> *"My first workshop had 4 people. Two were friends. One left early."*

---

**❌ The "Hey/So/Today" opener**
> *"Hey guys, so today I want to talk about..."*

Signals low-stakes content. Viewer marks the video as skippable.

**✅ Open mid-thought:**
> *"...and that's the third client this month who told me they wasted $5K on a course before finding me."*

---

### Body content mistakes

**❌ Multiple bodies (no clear structure)**

Script that has hook + topic 1 + topic 2 + topic 3 + sort of a CTA. Dilutes everything.

**✅ One framework, one structure:** Hook + Body + CTA (or PAS, BAB, etc.). Pick one framework. Don't blend.

---

**❌ Features over outcomes**
> *"This course has 12 modules and 40 lessons covering automation, integration, and deployment."*

Module count is irrelevant. Buyer doesn't want more content; they want results.

**✅ Outcomes over features:**
> *"Ship your first AI workflow this Saturday."*

---

**❌ Promise without proof**

Claims that aren't backed up later in the script. "This will change your life" with no evidence.

**✅ Every claim needs proof:** Specific number, named customer, screenshot, your own documented result.

---

**❌ Too much "we" and "I"**

Script that's 80% about you, 20% about the viewer.

**✅ 2:1 "you" ratio:** Count it. Rewrite until "you" appears twice as often as "I/we."

---

### CTA mistakes

**❌ Multiple CTAs**
> *"Sign up for the workshop! Also, follow me on Instagram! And subscribe to my newsletter!"*

Splits conversion across three actions. All three suffer.

**✅ One CTA, repeated:**
> *"Sign up for the workshop. Link in the description. Sign up now — doors close Friday."*

---

**❌ Buried CTA**

CTA only at the very end after 60 seconds of content. Viewers scrolled away.

**✅ CTA mentioned mid-script, repeated at end:** Once at 60% mark, once at the end. Two placements, same CTA.

---

**❌ Apologetic CTA**
> *"I hate this part, but if you want to work with me..."*

Signals the offer is bad. Confidence is part of the offer.

**✅ Confident transition:**
> *"If today resonated, here's how to keep going."*

---

**❌ Vague CTA**
> *"Learn more"*

What does that mean? Where do they go? Why now?

**✅ Specific CTA:**
> *"Sign up for Saturday's workshop. $17. Link below. Cohort closes Friday at midnight."*

---

### Voice and language mistakes

**❌ Hype words**

leverage, unlock, synergy, transformative, game-changing, revolutionary, next-level, cutting-edge, world-class.

**✅ Plain language:**
> Instead of "leverage AI to unlock next-level growth" → "use AI to do this faster"

---

**❌ Hedge words**

might, could possibly, sort of, kind of, maybe, perhaps.

**✅ Direct claims:**
> Instead of "this might help you save time" → "this saves you 6 hours/week"

(If you can't make the direct claim, the underlying claim isn't strong enough.)

---

**❌ Empty adverbs**

really, very, actually, literally, basically, just, simply, totally.

**✅ Cut them.** Test: does the sentence still make sense without the adverb? Yes → delete.

---

**❌ Business voice**

The formal register that creeps in when you write versus how you'd actually speak.

**Example:** *"This solution facilitates the streamlining of your workflow processes."*

**✅ Conversation voice:**
> *"This makes your work faster."*

Test: read it aloud. Sound like a press release? Rewrite.

---

### Format mistakes

**❌ Vertical video shot horizontally**

90% of Meta inventory is now vertical. Horizontal video wastes 50%+ of screen real estate on mobile.

**✅ Shoot 9:16 vertical for feed ads.** Always.

---

**❌ No captions**

85% of users watch with sound off. No captions = 85% of viewers can't follow.

**✅ Captions on every video.** Burned-in or auto-generated, both work.

---

**❌ Polished studio look for direct-response**

Professional video production lowers conversion for direct-response ads. Looks like an ad, gets treated like one.

**✅ Native, UGC-style:** phone-shot, looks like organic content. Outperforms polished consistently in 2026.

---

**❌ One ad, low budget, conclusion: "ads don't work"**

Running $20/day for 3 days isn't a test. It's reading tea leaves.

**✅ Minimum viable test:** $30-50/day per creative × 5-7 days × 3+ creative variations. Less than that, no conclusions.

---

### Webinar / workshop mistakes

**❌ 90% teaching, 10% pitch**

Feels generous; doesn't convert. The teaching should *prove* the offer is worth it; it shouldn't *be* the whole product.

**✅ 60-70% teaching, 30-40% offer + Q&A.** Tease the offer from the start so it doesn't feel like a surprise.

---

**❌ Surprise offer at minute 50**

Audience feels bait-and-switched.

**✅ Tease the offer in the first 5 minutes:** "By the end of this I'll show you how to do this 10x faster — stay until the end." Now the offer is expected.

---

**❌ Ending the moment the pitch ends**

Cuts off the 30-50% of buyers who buy in the Q&A.

**✅ Stay past scheduled end.** Buyers are the last to leave. The Q&A is the conversion event.

---

**❌ Different content every week**

Never refines into a tight script. Audience doesn't know what they're signing up for.

**✅ Same webinar every week, refined over time.** Russell Brunson ran his webinar 70+ times before automating. Repetition is the path to optimization.

---

### Funnel mistakes

**❌ Cold ad → $497 product**

Asks for too much commitment from someone who just met you.

**✅ Cold ad → lead magnet → email nurture → workshop → $497.** Stack micro-commitments.

---

**❌ Single launch, then silence**

You sell once during launch week, never again.

**✅ Evergreen funnel + periodic launches.** Lead magnet always running. Workshop weekly. Launch periodically for promotional pushes.

---

**❌ Lead magnet that's actually a pitch**

Bait-and-switch. Free thing is mostly an upsell. Burns reciprocity.

**✅ Embarrassingly useful lead magnet.** Genuinely valuable on its own. Pitch comes 3-5 emails later.

---

### Trust-destroying patterns

**❌ Fake scarcity**
> *"Only 3 spots left!"* (when there are unlimited)

**✅ Real scarcity, or no scarcity.** Cohort actually has 30 seats. Price actually goes up Friday.

---

**❌ Fake urgency**
> *"Price doubles in 24 hours!"* (when it doesn't)

**✅ Real urgency.** If you're going to claim urgency, follow through. Price actually changes.

---

**❌ Conspiracy framing**
> *"The gurus don't want you to know this..."*

Cheap; audiences are tired of it. Signals low quality.

**✅ Direct framing:**
> *"The conventional advice is wrong. Here's what the data actually shows."*

---

**❌ Stacking fake bonuses**
> *"Bonus #1: Lifetime access — value $999"* (when it costs nothing)

When the audience notices the fake stacking, trust collapses across the entire offer.

**✅ Real bonuses only.** If you're going to stack value, every item must have real standalone value.

---

### Things that worked 5 years ago but don't now

These were genuinely effective in 2018-2021 but have been so overused that audiences have built immunity. Avoid unless you have a fresh take:

- "You won't BELIEVE this..."
- "The secret nobody is talking about"
- "This one weird trick"
- "I'll never go back" closing line
- Reverse psychology ("This isn't for everyone...")
- "Stop scrolling" interruption
- Fake "wait — what?" reaction faces
- The "before/after" with vague before
- "If you're not ready, click away" hooks
- Story openers that telegraph the moral

If you use any of these, the audience pattern-matches to "low quality" and you lose them on the hook.

---

### Things that "feel" smart but don't convert

**❌ Three CTAs because "more chances to convert"**

Splits attention across all three. Lower total conversion.

**❌ Hiding the price to "book a call instead"**

Below $5K, transparent pricing converts better. Hiding feels like a trap.

**❌ Brand-first ad (logo + tagline, no value prop)**

Only works for established brands. You're not Nike. Lead with value.

**❌ Long intro to "set up" the value**

Viewers scroll. Front-load.

**❌ Saving the best for last**

Viewers don't reward you for hiding the point. They reward you for leading with it.

---

## Self-review checklist

Before delivering any script, run through this list. Any "yes" means rewrite that section.

- [ ] Does it open with "Hey," "So," "Today," or "What's up"?
- [ ] Does it contain any banned hype words?
- [ ] Are there empty adverbs that can be deleted?
- [ ] Is "I/we" used more than "you"?
- [ ] Are there multiple CTAs?
- [ ] Is the CTA only at the very end?
- [ ] Are claims unbacked by proof?
- [ ] Are there hedge words ("might," "could," "perhaps")?
- [ ] Does any sentence exceed 25 words?
- [ ] Does it sound like a press release when read aloud?
- [ ] Does the hook match one of the 7 named types in `hooks.md`?
- [ ] Is the framework one of the named frameworks in `frameworks.md`?
- [ ] Are there three distinct variations with three different hook types?
- [ ] Is there fake scarcity or fake urgency?
- [ ] Are any cliché openers used ("Are you struggling..." etc.)?
